## Graduate admissions and required documents

Sources: `JU_SRC_003`, `JU_SRC_004`

- The official Graduate page states that JU launched the Postgraduate Studies Department in 1993 to enable students to continue higher studies and receive graduate degrees through research meeting modern academic standards.
- The Graduate page exposes tabs for: Graduate Admission, How To Study, Required Documents, Graduation Conditions, Registration Mechanism, Research Progress Mechanism, The Jury Formation, and General Provisions.
- The Entrance Registration page exposes a Graduate Applicants tab.
- The extracted admission text for graduate/honors/diploma degrees states that applicants must hold a high school diploma, hold a bachelor's degree or compatible university degree from an accredited university/higher institute, complete required documents by deadline, and pay application fees.
- Required documents shown in extracted content include certified identification/passport, certified high school diploma, certified high school transcript for three years, certified bachelor's degree or equivalent and transcripts, two personal photos, and completed application/payment of registration fees.

## Graduate academic resources / research requirements

Source: `JU_SRC_005`

The Graduate Academic Resources page provides a Research Guide. Extracted Arabic content requests two electronic copies (CDs) in Word and PDF for the final thesis/dissertation after final committee-reader revisions, plus Arabic and English research abstracts. It also asks that the files open correctly and that the disc cover include thesis/dissertation title, faculty/department, stage, full name, university ID, and academic year.

## Tuition, financial system, and payment plans

Sources: `JU_SRC_006`, `JU_SRC_007`, `JU_SRC_011`

- Tuition & Fees page states fees are assessed per credit according to maximum/minimum semester credit hours for each major, with additional registration/services, housing, and Lebanese social security fees.
- Payment methods include cash at the university accounting department, cash at Byblos Bank branches, and international transfer to the university account.
- Financial System page for 2026-2027 states total semester fees are calculated by registered credits and cost per credit for the chosen major plus other fees.
- First payment amounts for Master's level: Public Health Genetics and Control of Infectious Diseases: `$400`; all other Master's majors: `$300`.
- First payment amounts for Doctoral level: Islamic Studies and Sharia: `$400`; Arabic Language and Literature: `$500`; Business Administration: `$600`.
- Installment deadlines shown: first payment Fall `03/09/2026`, Spring `03/02/2027`; second payment Fall `03/10/2026`, Spring `03/03/2027`; third payment Fall `03/11/2026`, Spring `03/04/2027`; fourth payment Fall `03/12/2026`, Spring `03/05/2027`.
- Foreign Students page provides international student fee sections, remedial foreign-language fees, and bank transfer details. Extracted content confirms tabs for `T.D. & Honors & M.A. Degree` and `Ph.D. Degree`, but the visible extracted table primarily shows other fees and undergraduate credit prices; exact graduate foreign-student credit prices were not fully exposed.

## Scholarships / financial aid

Source: `JU_SRC_008`

The Financial Aid page contains scholarship rules in Arabic. Extracted content states that JU allocates scholarships to different categories of enrolled students in different stages based on justified reasons; scholarship duration may be limited or cover the full prescribed stage; all scholarship percentages apply only to the value of study units and do not include semester or foreign-language fees; continuation requires good conduct and compliance with discipline rules; repeated courses are paid at the approved credit-unit fee; and students eligible for multiple scholarships benefit only from the highest one.
