# ESA Shared Graduate Data Report

Date accessed: 2026-07-07

## Admissions Summary

- ESA publishes a general admissions/orientation page for degree programs.
- Program-specific admissions criteria are published on the MBA, EMFM, MEMS, and DBA pages.
- The MBA page requires a university degree, minimum professional experience, GMAT or ESA MBA entrance exam, and a jury interview.
- EMFM and MEMS publish their own admissions criteria and experience requirements.
- No single graduate admissions handbook was confirmed across all programs.

## Required Documents Summary

- ESA does not expose one consolidated graduate checklist in the reviewed public sources.
- The public admissions and financial-aid documents confirm that supporting documents are required, but the exact graduate file varies by program and aid application.

## Language Requirements Summary

- The official FAQ states that graduate programs may be taught in English and French depending on the program.
- MBA courses are delivered in English and French.
- The Specialized Master in Marketing and Communication is delivered in French with some courses in English.
- No single university-wide graduate language threshold was confirmed.

## Tuition Model

- No university-wide numeric graduate tuition table was confirmed in the reviewed official ESA pages.
- Several program pages point to brochures for fee details, but those values were not normalized in this pass.
- Program tuition remains null in `research/esa/programs.json`.

## Tuition Mapping Result

- No program-level tuition rows were created.
- All graduate programs remain unmapped at the program level because no stable public numeric tuition table was captured.

## Scholarships / Financial Aid / Payment Summary

- ESA publishes financial-aid categories including scholarships, early-bird discounts, and solidarity financial aid.
- The official financial-aid page and PDF provide the solidarity-aid procedure and required documents.
- The published solidarity-aid text notes exclusions for EMBA and DBA.
- No detailed graduate payment-method matrix or installment-plan policy page was confirmed.

## Academic Calendar

- No university-wide graduate academic calendar was confirmed in the reviewed sources.
- Some program pages expose cohort-specific dates, but a central graduate calendar was not identified.

## Graduate Regulations

- The official FAQ and degree-program pages provide the available public guidance on duration, prerequisites, schedules, and delivery language.
- No standalone graduate regulations handbook was identified.

## International Students

- No dedicated graduate international-student policy page was confirmed.
- The reviewed sources mention international program components and partnerships, especially for MIM and GEMBA, but not a general international-student policy.

## Accreditation

- The MBA page references AMBA/BGA accreditation context.
- No broader university-wide graduate accreditation statement was isolated in the reviewed official sources.

- Graduate payment-method matrix
- Graduate installment-plan policy
- Consolidated graduate document checklist
- University-wide graduate calendar
