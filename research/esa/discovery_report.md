## Admissions evidence

- ESA has a general official admissions/orientation page for degree programs (`esa_admissions_en`).
- Program-specific admission criteria are available on several official pages:
  - MBA: university degree, minimum professional experience, GMAT or ESA MBA entrance exam, jury interview (`esa_mba_en`).
  - EMFM: Bachelor's degree with professional experience or Master's degree (`esa_emfm_en`).
  - MEMS: Bac+4/Master level and minimum professional experience (`esa_mems_fr`).
- The official FAQ page cross-checks prerequisites, duration, work-experience requirements, part-time schedule, and language notes across the graduate programs (`esa_faq_en`).

## Tuition, scholarships, and financial aid evidence

- Several program pages point to brochures for detailed tuition fees; not all tuition amounts are published directly on the official HTML pages inspected.
- Official financial aid page (`esa_financial_aid_en`) confirms ESA financial aid categories such as scholarships, early-bird devices/discounts, and social/solidarity financial aid, with exclusions for EMBA and DBA in the solidarity-aid text.
- Official financial aid PDF (`esa_financial_aid_pdf_2025_2026`) provides procedure and required documents for solidarity financial aid applications.
- MEMS and EMFM official pages explicitly mention Lebanese Citizens Scholarship and financial aid summaries.

## Language requirements / delivery language

The official FAQ page (`esa_faq_en`) includes language notes across programs. It states that some programs are taught in French and English, MBA courses are delivered in English and French, and the Specialized Master in Marketing and Communication is delivered in French with some courses in English.

## Academic calendar / important dates

No general graduate academic calendar page was confirmed in the official sources inspected. Program-specific pages may include dates. MEMS page includes 2026 dates for application/file submission and course start (`esa_mems_fr`).

## International students

No dedicated international-students page was confirmed in the inspected official sources. International tracks/exchange elements appear on program pages, especially MIM (`esa_mim_en`) and GEMBA (`esa_gemba_en`), but these are program components rather than a general international-student admissions policy.
